Financial Implication

Without the "Convergence" Product Development strategy, growth would fall toward 0.5% (legacy churn). The 2.5% Y1 growth projection is explicitly funded by upselling FWA/Fiber to 114M wireless connections — increasing switching costs and ARPU simultaneously.

Risk Audit & Model Integrity
Strategic Risk FlagsHIGH → MED → LOW

Frontier Integration Failure (HIGH): If $1B synergy target is missed, revenue growth falls toward sub-1%. Risk of execution on 30M+ fiber premise rollout across 25 states is material over the 2025–2028 integration window.

Debt Refinancing Risk (HIGH): $144B at 4.5% effective rate. A rise to 6%+ on refinancing would compress WACC advantage. Sensitivity: every 50bp WACC increase reduces intrinsic value by ~$15–20/share.

Price War with Cable MVNOs (MED): Comcast/Charter leverage cheap network access. If churn exceeds 1.5%, ARPA growth stalls, threatening the 2.5% Y1 revenue growth projection — the primary growth driver in this model.

Margin Expansion Risk (MED): 22%→23.5% EBIT margin path depends entirely on $5B cost-savings program executing on schedule. Legacy copper decommissioning has historically faced regulatory delays.

Regulatory / Spectrum Policy (LOW): FCC re-auctions possible. However, licenses held at $156B carrying value represent a sunk-cost moat — existing holdings are permanently protected by regulatory incumbency.
